# Problem Statement

Medical claims officers often work in environments where the volume of incoming claims is high, the turnaround expectations are strict, and the information needed for good decisions is spread across multiple documents and systems. In practice, that means an officer may need to review dozens or hundreds of claims while simultaneously checking coding consistency, billing patterns, claim history, supporting documents, and workflow requirements.

The challenge is not only organizational efficiency. It is also the day-to-day experience of the reviewer.

- Large claim volumes create pressure to move quickly.
- Manual review steps make it difficult to apply the same depth of attention to every claim.
- Delayed visibility into unusual patterns means higher-risk claims may stay mixed in with routine claims.
- Historical searching can be slow, especially when the reviewer needs to look for similar member, provider, diagnosis, or billing patterns.
- Repeated checks across documents and prior submissions can increase fatigue and inconsistency.

For a medical claims officer, the result is decision pressure without enough timely context. A reviewer may know that a claim needs more attention, but still spend valuable time piecing together why.

ClaimGuard is framed as a response to that operational reality. The prototype is designed to help a claims officer identify which claims deserve attention first, understand the review signals behind that prioritization, and keep human judgment at the center of the process. Instead of replacing review, the goal is to reduce friction in review.

The problem this prototype addresses is simple:

How can a medical claims officer get faster, clearer, and more explainable risk prioritization for health claims without losing human control over the decision?

## Why This Prototype Matters Now

The MVP shows that a claims team does not need to begin with a black-box prediction system to improve review quality. A practical first step is an explainable triage layer that:

- brings review signals into one queue
- shows why a claim was prioritized
- supports maker-checker escalation
- keeps an audit trail of reviewer actions
- leaves the final decision with the claims officer

That makes ClaimGuard suitable as a fellowship prototype, demo solution, and starting point for a carefully governed pilot.
