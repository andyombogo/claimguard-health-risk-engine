# User Persona

## Primary Persona

**Role:** Medical Claims Officer  
**Environment:** Health insurance claims review team  
**Core responsibility:** Review incoming claims, identify items that require closer verification, and make or support timely claim decisions.

## Persona Snapshot

The medical claims officer works under volume and time pressure. They are expected to review claims accurately, identify unusual patterns early, and document the reasoning behind escalations or approvals. Their job depends on balancing speed, consistency, and fairness.

They do not need a system that makes the final decision for them. They need a system that helps them see the right claim first, understand why it may need attention, and move through review work with better context.

## What This User Needs

- Fast access to recent claim history for a member or provider
- Clear visibility into duplicate patterns, missing documents, abnormal billing, and diagnosis-treatment mismatches
- A concise explanation of why a claim is being prioritized
- A queue that surfaces higher-risk claims before routine ones
- Workflow support for maker-checker review and escalation

## Pain Points

- Manual searching through historical claims and scattered records
- Review delays caused by incomplete or fragmented information
- Decision pressure when many claims appear similar at first glance
- Supporting documents arriving in inconsistent formats or incomplete bundles
- Repeating the same checks across multiple claims with limited automation

## Success Criteria

- Sees potentially risky claims earlier in the queue
- Gets understandable explanations instead of opaque scores
- Can review provider and member patterns without extensive manual lookup
- Keeps human control over the final decision
- Can document why a claim was reviewed, returned, escalated, or approved

## Design Implications For ClaimGuard

- Prioritize explainability over complexity
- Present risk indicators in reviewer-friendly language
- Support consistent triage without removing professional judgment
- Make the workflow feel assistive rather than accusatory
